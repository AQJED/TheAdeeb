Why This Blog Exists

Most “best practices” articles assume you have a full team, QA, and time to experiment. A lot of real integration work doesn’t look like that.

This blog exists because I learned most lessons the hard way. I made the classic first-timer mistakes: wrong assumptions about byte order, type sizes, packet layouts, “clean” networks, and “safe” dev machines. When the other side is a black box, those mistakes get expensive fast.

So I write these posts to document what actually went wrong, what I assumed, what broke, and what fixed it. The goal is simple: help beginners in cross-platform programming, and engineers integrating with existing hardware or software, avoid my rocky path and ship something reliable sooner.
